Buried deep in a [blog comment from Shtetl-optimized in 2011](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=755), I found [a wonderful essay from 2007](https://leidlmair.at/doc/WhyHeideggerianAIFailed.pdf) by Hubert Dreyfus discussing why rationalist AI failed 

Rules-based and symbol-based AI failed because it's trying to code truth. It's trying to discover truth. And do metaphysics.

turning away from metaphysics into phenomenology 

Is logic evolved or real? if it evolved, it shouldn't fit the world. if it's real, it should.

But we evolved. is language better fitting the world, or is math?

language currently seems to be well-fitted to the world, math seems to be better-fitted to the world

language maps onto the world in subtlety because humans are neural- when we don't have a sufficiently closely-wrapped term or description for a situation, we co-create new terms that fit into the massive language net. math is sort of created by humans, but the creation is much more constrained along rigid lines of existing definitions- you end up proving siblings of the same axioms in every class of mathematical object, it's so much more discrete, the categories are so much better-defined. you can't insert an extra integer between three and four, that is nonsensical.

don't disrespect language, don't disrespect math either

Richard Rorty said we're beyond morality and need to focus on the consequences, which is an outwards-facing observational morality... write here more about rorty and morality and what it means in this paradigm for ai alignment and RLHF]

much of lesswrong seems to be a little bit stuck in this paradigm... hume obsessives, they reify game theory. read anti-yudkowsky for even more of a critique along these lines...

heidegger's question concerning technology- another sense of heideggerian AI- thinking about human language and human experience (vision, sound, multimodal models, capturing data from all our apps, human ai) and objectifying it into something to be *used* and trained on. very strange! data market is extremely strange. using our own data, but the big corporations were already using our data, but now it's flooding into everyone's life. what does it mean to life a quantized life like this?
